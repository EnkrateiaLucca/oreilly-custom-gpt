# Plan Your Literature Review

## Source
https://claude.com/resources/use-case/plan-your-literature-review

## Template Prompt

I'm beginning a literature review on {RESEARCH_TOPIC} and its role in {SPECIFIC_ASPECT}, specifically {FOCUS_CONDITIONS}. Can you help me:

1. Search {DATABASE|PubMed,Google Scholar,Web of Science,Scopus} for relevant papers
2. Prioritize papers based on {CRITERIA|relevance,impact factor,recency,citation count}
3. Organize findings by {ORGANIZATION_METHOD|study type,chronology,methodology,themes}
4. Create a beautifully designed {OUTPUT_FORMAT|Word document,PDF,HTML page} that serves as my research roadmap

Please include papers from the last {TIME_FRAME|5 years,10 years,decade} and focus on {STUDY_TYPES|systematic reviews,meta-analyses,clinical trials,observational studies}.

## Use Case Description

This template helps researchers systematically plan literature reviews by searching academic databases, evaluating paper relevance, organizing findings, and creating professional documentation. The workflow emphasizes moving beyond mechanical sorting toward synthesis and original analysis.

## Variables Explanation

- **RESEARCH_TOPIC**: Main subject of research (e.g., "the gut-brain axis")
- **SPECIFIC_ASPECT**: Particular relationship or mechanism being studied
- **FOCUS_CONDITIONS**: Specific conditions or outcomes of interest (e.g., "depression and anxiety")
- **DATABASE**: Academic database to search
- **CRITERIA**: How to prioritize the literature found
- **ORGANIZATION_METHOD**: How to structure the findings
- **OUTPUT_FORMAT**: Desired format for the research roadmap
- **TIME_FRAME**: How far back to search
- **STUDY_TYPES**: Types of studies to prioritize
